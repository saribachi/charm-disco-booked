Paid Ads, Concept Board

Creative directions for paid acquisition. Every ad drives to the VSL thank-you pages at go.hirecharm.com, so the page is the destination and the ad is the hook. Two tracks: GTM on LinkedIn plus cold-email-audience Meta, CS-Flex on Meta and IG for D2C operators.

The through-line for every ad

Charm is a full-service agency. We get the leads. We build the infrastructure. We write the words. Your only job is to decide what you want to say.

Be principled about this: Charm is an agency, not a tool, a sequencer, or an infrastructure subscription you run yourself. Real people do the work, end to end. So infrastructure is never a standalone pitch, it is one of three things a team handles for you. The strongest hook reframes the whole thing as creative freedom: focus on what to say, not how to get there to say it.

Status: concepts only. Nothing is shot, built, or running yet. The mockups below are directional. They use the same media still owed by Chris (founder VSL footage doubles as founder-to-camera ad source; testimonials feed the testimonial-rotation format). Hold any paid launch until the real VSL and Q&A videos replace the placeholders on the pages.

Four lanes in play: static creative mockups (see below) · founder-to-camera hooks · podcast-style conversation clips · competitor reference. Creative follows the official brandbook (Poppins, black + purple family, sparkle mark, violet/magenta glow). The blue/green you see on the GTM and CS-FLEX badges is funnel navigation, not the master brand. Style rules apply: no em dashes, salty-dry not fruity, wizard frame sprinkled.

🎙️ Podcast-Style Ad Approach

The format: a recorded conversation (Chris solo to camera, or Chris plus a guest/host) that feels native to the feed, not like an ad. Record 20 to 40 minutes around one topic, then clip into 30 to 90 second units. It buys authority and trust the static creative can't, and one good sit-down yields a dozen ads.

How to use this board: each card is a conversation starter, not a script. It gives a thesis to argue, a cold-open line to record first (that line becomes the ad's first 3 seconds), and the clippable moments to listen for while talking. Pick one, hit record, talk like a human. Salty-dry, wizard frame sprinkled, no em dashes.

GTM topics GTM

The creative angle

"If you could reach every decision maker, what would you say?"

The reframe topic. Most founders pour all their energy into the how (lists, tools, deliverability) and never get to the what. Flip it: if getting in front of everyone were solved, what's the actual message? That's the only thing worth your time. We handle the rest.
Cold open: "Here's a question almost nobody can answer fast: if you could get in front of every decision maker in your market tomorrow, what would you actually say?"
Listen for clips: the question itself · "focus on what to say, not how to get there" · the three-things-as-one-system reveal.
The whole system

"Leads, infrastructure, words. Why they have to be one thing."

The unifier topic. Each piece on its own fails: great words that land in spam, perfect infrastructure with no one to email, a clean list nobody writes to. The value isn't any single piece, it's owning all three as one pipeline so nothing falls between the seams.
Cold open: "People hire us for infrastructure, or for leads, or for copy. The truth is you can't buy one of those and win. Here's why."
Listen for clips: the "falls between the seams" insight · the three-into-one explainer · "you just show up."
Myth-bust

"Cold email isn't dead. You just keep killing it."

The whole "cold email is dead" take is wrong. It's not dead, it's an infrastructure discipline now, and most people are still treating it like a copywriting exercise.
Cold open: "Everybody tells me cold email is dead. It's not dead. You torched your own domain and called it a funeral."
Listen for clips: the "infrastructure discipline" reframe · the warmup explainer · the one-line mic-drop.
War story

"We sent 500 emails from one domain so you don't have to."

Walk through the self-sabotage cycle as a story: the DIY founder, the main domain, the blacklist, the "can't email my own mom" punchline. Funny, relatable, sets up done-for-you.
Cold open: "There's a specific moment a founder realizes they blacklisted their own company. Let me describe it."
Listen for clips: the blacklist moment · the cost math · "or you let people who do this every day handle it."
Behind the curtain

"What 'done for you' actually means."

Demystify the machine: domains, warmup, data pipelines, the human who writes the words. The unglamorous magic. People buy the thing they never want to think about again.
Cold open: "When we say done for you, here's the boring list of things you'll never think about again."
Listen for clips: the "10x the value" line · the boring-list montage · the sleep-while-it-runs beat.
Contrarian / AI

"What everyone gets wrong about AI in outbound."

The market is drowning in AI hype. The contrarian truth: AI makes the machine possible, but the reputation, deliverability, and judgment are still the moat. Lean into "what the gurus skip."
Cold open: "Everyone's selling you AI that sends more email. More email is exactly what got you blacklisted."
Listen for clips: the hype-vs-moat split · the "gurus skip this" insider beat · a sharp one-liner.

Competitor Ad Review

Reference points from Chris's review of competitor ads. The spectrum runs scrappy-and-unscripted (Listkit, Leadgenjay) to polished-and-data-heavy (Hypergen). We can pull from both lanes.

Listkit peer / data aggregator

  • Static photo plus selfie-style talking video, simple CTA, very unscripted.
  • "No visuals, just speaking" angle: juggling tools, "all you have to do is manage replies."
  • Lightly edited, subtitles, own-case-study angle: "we have so many more leads than most other data aggregators, so you'll never run out."
  • Static image plus screenshot proof.

Takeaway: low-production, unscripted founder talk plus screenshot proof. Cheap to replicate and a direct fit for our founder-to-camera angles.

Leadgenjay AI automation / lead gen

  • "Introducing employees that never sleep," AI-automation framing.
  • Specific offer plus scarcity: "limited availability and price going up."
  • Origin story: "why I built X system," "I built it for people like you."
  • "What everyone gets wrong about AI" (we can lean into how much is now possible).
  • Results-while-sleeping: "we generated X leads in X time while sleeping," "most people are still manually scraping LinkedIn."
  • Clips from client calls reading their testimonials, rotating the faces you see.
  • Static comparison visuals.
  • "You know what's going to work, you just need people to build it for you."

Takeaway: heavy on origin-story, contrarian-AI, and testimonial-face rotation. Their "what everyone gets wrong about AI" maps directly to our infrastructure-reveal and podcast contrarian topics.

Hypergen premium / data-heavy

  • Classier, more data-heavy, branding-consistent.
  • Very HubSpot-style branding and stock imagery.

Takeaway: the polished end of the spectrum. If we want a premium-brand lane (the Ferrari positioning), this is the reference, contrasted against Listkit's scrappy style. Charm can sit in the middle: premium look, unscripted founder voice.
