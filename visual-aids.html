Discovery Call Visual Aid Blueprints

Draft renderings of the slides Chris deploys mid-call. These are V1 content blueprints. The message and the moment each slide serves are locked; a designer polishes later. Each slide has a spec card: which phase of the call it appears in, the line Chris says over it, and what still needs to be filled in.

5 · The Seasonality Problem

CS

The CS equivalent of the blur slide: the visual argument that a fixed team can't track variable demand. In-house hiring is a staircase that's always late; Charm's capacity is a curve that hugs demand.

Why hiring can't solve this

Demand isn't fixed. Your team is.

Your support volume In-house team (hiring staircase) Charm flexible capacity
Flexible capacity isn't a perk. It's the whole point.

Call phase

During the read (Phase 7) or in the remedy right after the Three Things slide. Strongest when the prospect just described a seasonal blowup. Point at the spike and say "that's your November."

Talk line

"This black line is your volume. The red line is what hiring looks like: always late, never enough, then you're overstaffed in January. We're the green line. We just track your demand."

To finalize

  • Swap in a real (anonymized) client volume curve when one exists

6 · How This Works From Here

Both tracks

The close-to-proposal visual. Shows the prospect exactly where they are and that the next step is small and concrete: a proposal call, not a contract. Reinforces the read and remedy frame: the read today, the remedy next.

Where we are

Two calls. That's the whole process.

You are here

This call: the read

You talk, we listen. We get our read on what's actually in the way of where you're trying to go.

3–5 days later

Proposal call: the remedy

We walk you through exactly what we'd take off your plate, the scope, and the investment.

If it's a fit

Go live

We build, we run it, you watch it work. Premium and repeatable: the same machine, every time.

Call phase

Phase 10, shown while Chris says "let me pull up my calendar." Makes the ask feel like the natural next square, not a commitment.

Talk line

"Here's the whole process. There's no maze. Today was the read. Next call I show you the remedy. Let me pull up my calendar and we'll find a time this week."

To finalize

  • Confirm the 3–5 day proposal turnaround is the real commitment
  • "Go live" copy carries the Ferrari frame ("same machine, every time"), Chris to confirm wording

7 · State of the Market

Both tracks

New v2 opening slide: align on a shared worldview before any pitch. The operator is caught between two anxieties; Chris names both, gets the nod, then delivers the turn.

Where every operator is right now

Caught between two things

Operational anxiety

"Should we be using these AI tools? At what velocity? What we knew six months ago is obsolete. Daily."

😬
The opportunity of a generation, and the fear of screwing it up

Cost anxiety

"What does adopting this cost? What does NOT adopting it cost? Either answer is expensive."

The turn: "Let me show you how we unify your whole cycle in the middle of this."

Phase 3, right after the agenda yes, before any discovery. Pause on this slide until they visibly agree.

Talk line
